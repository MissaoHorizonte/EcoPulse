Use of Technology

Our project facilitates access to healthcare in peripheral communities and for people with disabilities, using simple devices that communicate medical needs. This innovative device enables continuous health monitoring for high-risk groups, operating 24/7. It serves as an essential tool for strengthening public health, directly contributing to the population’s quality of life by preventing severe complications and enabling faster medical interventions. In addition to real-time vital sign tracking, the smartwatch is designed to serve vulnerable communities, ensuring accessibility, sustainability, and reliability.

  • Real-time monitoring: continuous measurement of blood pressure, heart rate, blood oxygen levels, and fall detection.
  • Automatic alerts: immediate transmission of anomalies via radio waves, ensuring communication even in areas with limited infrastructure.
  • Integration with mobile units: direct connection with mobile health stations, enabling quick and targeted medical response.
  • Sustainability: manufactured with recyclable materials, reducing environmental impact.
  • Focus on vulnerable populations: specifically designed for peripheral areas and communities exposed to extreme climate conditions.

About the Project

Our project aims to improve access to healthcare and promote sustainability in Uberlândia, focusing on vulnerable areas with limited infrastructure that are sensitive to extreme climate variations. The lack of accessible healthcare facilities and the worsening of health conditions due to climate change are critical challenges. To mitigate these problems, we propose an integrated system that involves the distribution of smart watches to monitor the health of vulnerable populations. These devices, which measure blood pressure, heart rate, blood oxygenation, and detect falls, help prevent serious complications and enable rapid intervention. In case of anomalies, the watch sends an alert via radio waves to the healthcare system, triggering mobile health stations. These mobile stations will be deployed seasonally to the regions most in need, based on detailed data on climate and extreme environmental conditions, such as air quality, extreme temperatures, and other climatic factors. This ensures that medical care is optimized for areas at greatest risk of health problems due to climatic conditions. In addition, the clocks will be made from recyclable materials, promoting the city's sustainability. The system aims not only to improve the efficiency of medical care, but also to offer a response tailored to the needs of each community, preventing health complications and ensuring rapid action when most needed.

How It Works

  1. User triggers the device (buttons) or the device identifies health critical situations.
  2. The device generates a radio signal notifying close health care centers.
  3. Medical centers receive the alert and provide care (Mobile health units).
  4. Displays health information like, oxygen levels, blood pressure and pulse.
